The old lighthouse keeper, Silas, squinted at the churning sea. The storm, a snarling beast, had been raging for hours, and the rhythmic pulse of the lamp was his only companion in the howling darkness. He'd seen a thousand storms, but this one felt different, a primal fury that unsettled even his weathered soul. A distant flash of lightning briefly illuminated the raging waves, revealing a small, tossed-about boat struggling against the monstrous swells. Silas gripped his mug, his knuckles white, a silent prayer forming on his lips.
